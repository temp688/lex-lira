burrito lore

So basically a guy named Juan Méndez was a street vendor on the mean streets of Chihuahua Mexico, but one day he was like yo dawg, “i'm tired of eating tacos, I need something different” and so he grabbed all the ingredients required. He decided to wrap the food in a delectable tortilla to keep the food warm and pretty tasty while he transported it on his trusty/noble steed aka a normal donkey. But then he was like yo what if i keep the food wrapped in the tortilla because why not. And so he did. No one is sure if burrito was named after the donkey.

recipe

  1. Cook the beef in a skillet. Next saulté onions in olive oil till it's golden, next let the beef brown then stir for more flavor.
  2. Stir in some taco seasoning and enchilada sauce. Let it simmer until it is thick. Scrape the pan with sauce to add some more flavor into the mix.
  3. Cook cilantro-lime rice then let the rice sit in the heat after cooking for a fluffy texture.
  4. Spread rice beef, refried beans and cheese around and evenly around the tortilla make sure not to add too many ingredients so it can close and so it can roll easily.
  5. Roll the burrito tightly so it does not fall apart, make sure to roll it on a greasy sheet and bake it till it is crisp put light oil spray before cooling the burrito
  6. Serve and allow the burrito recipe to cool then apply your toppings of choice
